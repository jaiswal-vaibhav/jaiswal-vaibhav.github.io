About

I am a senior researcher at the neutronics and criticality safety department of the French Authority for Nuclear Safety and Radiation Protection (ASNR). I have more than ten years of experience in nuclear data, covering differential experiments, nuclear data evaluation, data processing, and benchmarking for reactor physics and criticality safety studies.

My research work began at ASNR (then IRSN), where I carried out a PhD on the development of thermal scattering cross-section data. In this work, I performed double-differential time-of-flight measurements and conducted atomistic simulations to produce high-fidelity nuclear data for neutron transport applications. After completing my PhD, I continued my research as a post-doctoral fellow at the French Alternative Energies and Atomic Energy Commission (CEA), and later returned to ASNR as a researcher.

I hold a bachelor's degree in Physics and an M. Tech in Nuclear Science and Technology from the University of Delhi, India, as well as a master's degree in Nuclear Engineering from the French National Institute for Nuclear Science and Technology (INSTN) and CentraleSup√©lec, one of France's leading engineering schools.

My expertise lies in nuclear data development, qualification, and the integration of evaluated data into neutron transport simulations. I also work on uncertainty quantification, sensitivity analysis, and the development and processing of covariance matrices to support the propagation of uncertainties in neutronic calculations. I collaborate with research institutes and international partners, contributing to the JEFF project and several expert groups. I have contributed to national and international R&D initiatives, and supervise PhD students, postdoctoral researchers, and interns working in reactor physics and neutronics.
