About Me

Dr. Vaibhav Jaiswal is an experienced research engineer at ASNR, the French authority for nuclear safety and radioprotection. His work centers on advancing nuclear cross section data and Monte Carlo simulations, with particular emphasis on thermal scattering for moderator materials. Bolstered by a PhD in Nuclear Engineering and extensive international collaborations, he contributes to cutting-edge solutions in reactor physics, nuclear safety, and data evaluation.

Originally from India, Dr. Jaiswal obtained a Bachelor’s in Physics and an M.Tech in Nuclear Science and Technology at the University of Delhi. Seeking broader exposure to nuclear sciences, he relocated to France, where he earned a specialized Master’s in Nuclear Engineering from the National Institute for Nuclear Science and Technology (INSTN) and subsequently completed his PhD at the University of Lille. His doctoral work on time-of-flight experiments and atomistic simulations continues to inform his efforts in refining thermal scattering cross section data for research and criticality safety.

An active participant in the Joint Evaluated Fission and Fusion (JEFF) community, Dr. Jaiswal frequently engages with international teams to share findings and shape global nuclear research. He has presented his work at major conferences, secured European funding for nuclear data projects, and led collaborative studies involving organizations like Oak Ridge National Laboratory (US). With a commitment to mentorship, he also supervises graduate students and interns, blending technical expertise with a passion for fostering the next generation of nuclear scientists.

Research Topics

  • Nuclear Cross Section Data

    Investigating the fundamental interactions of neutrons within nuclear reactors, focusing on the measurement, processing, and evaluation of nuclear cross section data essential for reactor design and safety analysis.

  • Thermal Scattering Law

    Developing thermal scattering laws for various materials to accurately simulate neutron behavior at thermal energies, improving the fidelity of neutron transport calculations.

  • Uncertainty Analysis

    Assessing the impact of uncertainties in nuclear data on reactor physics calculations, contributing to the optimization of nuclear safety and performance.

  • Neutronics

    Advancing the field of neutronics through detailed simulations and experiments to enhance the understanding of neutron behavior and reactor kinetics.

  • Criticality Safety Research

    Focusing on the prevention of accidental criticality in nuclear facilities by analyzing and ensuring the safe handling, storage, and disposal of fissile materials.
